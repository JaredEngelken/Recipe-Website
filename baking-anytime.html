Anytime Bakes

Pretzels

Amount:

  • 10-12 Pretzels

Time:

  • 2-3 Hours

Ingredients:

  • 17.64 oz - Type 550 baking flour
  • 1.27 cups - milk
  • 1 tsp - normal salt
  • 1/2 cube or .75 oz - cake yeast
  • 1 tsp - sugar
  • 7 tbs - lard,butter, or margarine
  • 10 pinches - coarse salt
  • 4.25 cups - water
  • 3 tbs - baking soda
  • 1 - large egg

Instructions:

  1. Mix the yeast with the sugar and several drops of room temperature or slightly warm milk in a bowl with room for expansion.
  2. Stir the yeast mixture together into a liquid solution. Let it sit for 15 minutes at room temperature.
  3. Soften the lard, butter, or margarine, but do not melt it.
  4. Mix the flour, normal salt, softened butter, lard, or margarine, the remaining milk, and the yeast solution together in a large bowl.
  5. Cover the dough with a dry towel and set it in a warm place for 30 minutes. The dough should double in size.
  6. Gently knead the dough and separate into 10 - 20 2-inch diameter balls.
  7. Gently roll the dough balls into 1-foot length sausages, being slightly thicker in the middle.
  8. Shape the dough sausages into pretzels by twisting the two ends together and then laying them over the middle section. Let the pretzels sit for 15 minutes.
  9. Place the pretzels in the refrigerator for about 1 hour so the dough becomes firm.
  10. Before the hour is up, boil the water in a large pot, allowing enough depth to submerge the pretzels individually.
  11. Add the baking soda to the water so it dissolves.
  12. Grease a baking pan and preheat the oven to 425° F.
  13. Crack the egg into a bowl and whisk the yolk into the albumen until it is well mixed and frothy. Set the bowl aside.
  14. Remove the pretzels from the refrigerator and place them individually in the boiling water solution for about 30 seconds each. They will float to the surface shortly before they are ready to be removed.
  15. Carefully remove the pretzels from the water with a slotted spatula and lay them on the well greased baking pan.
  16. Batter the pretzels liberally with the egg mixture and then sprinkle the coarse salt over them to taste.
  17. Place the pretzels into a cold oven and bake them at 425° F for 18 - 20 minutes or until they are golden-brown.
  18. Remove from the oven and enjoy with a beer!

    19. Optional:

    • Lay a slice of cheese on top of the pretzels before baking.
    • Use sugar and cinnamon on top instead of salt for something sweeter.
    • Lay a slice of cheese on top and sprinkle bacon or diced ham and chives on top before baking.

Sourdough Pretzels

Amount:

  • 12 Servings

Time:

  • 2 1/2 Hours

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbs - soft butter
  • 2 tbs - sugar
  • 1 cup - warm milk
  • 1 1/2 cups - active sourdough starter
  • 4 cups - all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbs - baking soda
  • 12 cups - water
  • 1 - egg
  • 2 tsp - salt

Instructions:

  1. Warm the milk (to 110 degrees F) and it add to a mixing boul with the butter, sugar, and sourdough starter.
  2. Add the flour and mix well until the dough forms a soft ball. Turn it out onto a lightly floured counter and knead for 2-3 minutes. Then form the dough into a smooth ball and place it into a lightly-oiled bowl.
  3. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a moistened towel and allow it to rest/rise for 2 hours.
  4. Once the dough has risen, turn it out onto a lightly floured counter. Punch the dough down and knead lightly for a brief minute or two. Then separate the dough into 12 equal pieces.
  5. Roll out each piece into a long strip 26-30" in length. If you are slow to roll out the pieces, then cover the balls of dough with a damp cloth to prevent them from drying out.
  6. Once the dough strips have been rolled out, shape them into a pretzel and place them on a parchment-lined cookie sheet. You will need to use 2-3 cookie sheets.
  7. Place the cookie sheets in the freezer for 20-30 minutes. The goal is to allow the pretzels to harden just enough that they can be dunked in the water bath.
  8. With the pretzels in the freezer, preheat the oven to 450 degrees F. Fill a large gallon stockpot 3/4 of the way with water; bring it to a strong simmer. Add the baking soda and stir to dissolve it.
  9. Remove the pretzels from the freezer; add them to the water a few at a time so they don't stick together.
  10. Allow the pretzels to simmer until they float to the top. Gently remove them from their bath and place them back on the cookie sheet.
  11. Brush with a beaten egg and sprinkle with your choice of toppings. Bake at 450 degrees F for 14-15 minutes until lightly brown.
  12. Remove the pretzels and allow to cool before enjoying with a beer!

    13. Optional:

    • Lay a slice of cheese on top of the pretzels before baking.
    • Use sugar and cinnamon on top instead of salt for something sweeter.
    • Lay a slice of cheese on top and sprinkle bacon or diced ham and chives on top before baking.
